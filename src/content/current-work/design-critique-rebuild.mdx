## The problem

When I joined, design critique was a 90-minute weekly meeting where the
loudest opinion in the room usually won. Junior designers stopped bringing
early work because the cost of being wrong in front of the team was too high.
Work was polished before it was ever critiqued — which meant the most
expensive kind of feedback, the kind that changes direction, was arriving
far too late to matter.

## What I changed

1. **Async-first, written critique.** Designers post work-in-progress with a
   specific question attached — not "thoughts?" but "I'm deciding between
   A and B, here's the tradeoff I see." This forces clarity before the room
   weighs in.
2. **Explicit decision rights.** Every project has a named decider. Critique
   informs the decision; it doesn't replace it. This killed the
   death-by-committee dynamic.
3. **A 30-minute, biweekly live session** reserved only for genuinely
   contested calls — not status updates.
